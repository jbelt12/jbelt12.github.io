When will the Baltimore Orioles
be competitive again?

The Baltimore Orioles have been a somewhat non-competitive baseball team for the past 20 seasons, besides a six-year span from 2012-2016 that saw them reach the postseason three times and win the American League East division in 2014. Since the 2017 season, the Orioles have had the worst record in the MLB twice and the second worst once.

This has caused many fans, writers, and executives across the league to take a step back and look at how non-competitive this team has been. Since the 2019 hiring of Mike Elias as the executive vice president and general manager of the team, the Orioles have been looked at as a team that will eventually contend in upcoming years. Elias is known best for digging the Houston Astros out of a similar hole and leading them to a World Series championship in 2017 through the draft and his ability to groom young talent.

Let’s look at how Elias can do the same thing in Baltimore.

The Orioles were one of the MLB’s best teams from 2012-2016, leading the league in home runs in 2012, 2014, and 2016. Here's a look at the league leaders in home runs from the first year the Orioles made the playoffs until this past season.

As you can see, the Orioles had a player lead the league in home runs from 2013-2017.

After starting 32-75 in 2018, they had a fire sale at the trade deadline and blew the team up, trading Manny Machado, Jonathan Schoop, Kevin Gausman and multiple other players for prospects.

They then hired Mike Elias in the offseason to come rebuild this team.

Since joining the Orioles in 2019, Elias has conducted a complete overhaul of the team, on the field and off it. Elias is best known for his turnaround of Houston, taking them from worst to first in five plus seasons. His use of analytics and hiring tactics were unheard of back when he took over in Houston in 2012, but as we’ve seen over the recent years with Tampa Bay, they have become very prominent.

As far as the Orioles are concerned, it looks like there is a lot to be excited about for the future in Baltimore.

Orioles Top-15 Prospects

These 15 players are just the beginning of what is to come over the next few years in Baltimore, as their lack of spending in free agency and draft selections are finally seeming like they will pan out as planned.

As far as the present is concerned, there is a little less to be excited about.

Orioles 2021 Hitting Leaders

Players like Ryan Mountcastle , Cedric Mullins and Trey Mancini had great years statistically and led the team in most offensive categories, but the productivity pretty much stops there.

These main producers are in the future plans of the Orioles (so it seems) and will open the door for prospects like Adley Rutschman, Gunnar Henderson and Colton Cowser to shoot through the minor leagues and into Baltimore to provide them help as soon as possible.

As far as pitching goes, there is a little more work to be done.

Orioles 2021 Pitching Statistics

Aside from John Means -- who has been in trade talks recently -- there is not much to be excited about on the mound for the Orioles. Luckily, the farm system has some great young arms that will be making their debuts very soon.

Guys like Grayson Rodriguez and D.L Hall are looked at as pitchers who can make an impact as soon as 2021 or 2022.

The Future

There has been a lot of talk about the future for the Orioles, and for good reason. The recipe to success is right in front of them and a proven leader is at the helm to guide them through it. As long as they can develop this talent, keep them healthy, and spend some money, the Orioles are going to be a team that contends very soon.
